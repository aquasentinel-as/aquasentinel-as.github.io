⚙️ Our Technology

Where intelligent hardware meets real-time water monitoring.

Working Principle

Aqua Sentinel operates using a network of embedded sensors connected to a NodeMCU ESP8266. The microcontroller collects real-time data from all sensors, processes it, and transmits it wirelessly to the Aqua Sentinel Dashboard, where the data is analyzed and visualized for decision-making.

Hardware Components

NodeMCU ESP8266

The central microcontroller that collects sensor data and sends it to the web interface via Wi-Fi.

I2C Module

Facilitates communication between the NodeMCU and LCD with reduced wiring complexity.

LCD 16x2 Display

Displays live readings directly from sensors for on-site viewing and verification.

LED Indicators

Green and Red LEDs indicate safe or unsafe water quality conditions in real-time.

Push Switch

Used for resetting or calibrating sensors during testing or field operations.

Breadboard & Wires

Enable flexible connections between components for easy prototyping and debugging.

Sensors Used

pH Sensor

Monitors acidity and alkalinity levels to maintain safe water conditions.

TDS Sensor

Measures dissolved salts and minerals to determine water purity.

Turbidity Sensor

Analyzes water clarity and detects suspended particles.

Dissolved Oxygen Sensor

Tracks oxygen levels essential for aquatic organisms.

Temperature Sensor

Monitors water temperature to ensure stable environmental conditions.

Heavy Metal Sensor

Detects presence of toxic elements like lead, mercury, and arsenic in water.

Data Flow Architecture

Sensor readings are captured by the NodeMCU and displayed locally on the LCD. The ESP8266 then transmits the same data through Wi-Fi to the Aqua Sentinel dashboard, where it is processed, color-coded, and displayed for users to interpret instantly.

Future Integrations

Aqua Sentinel is designed with scalability in mind. Our goal is to evolve beyond local monitoring into a globally connected, intelligent water quality ecosystem powered by data and AI.

Cloud Data Storage

Future updates will enable long-term data logging on cloud servers for trend analysis and reporting.

AI-Based Prediction

Machine learning algorithms will predict contamination patterns and alert authorities before crises occur.

Mobile App Integration

Users will be able to view real-time data and alerts directly on their smartphones through an Android app.

IoT Network Expansion

Multiple Aqua Sentinel units can be connected regionally to form an integrated water safety network.
